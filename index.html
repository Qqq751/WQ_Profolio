Project Focus / Analysis

The Intersection of Tactile Art and Digital Identity.

WQ Jewelry is not just a brand; it is an exploration of emotional textures. In this project, the core challenge was translating the raw, irregular beauty of handcrafted metalwork into a clean, modern digital language. The brand identity utilizes an intertwined 'W' and 'Q' monogram that mirrors the organic structures of the jewelry themselves. By contrasting high-contrast editorial typography with minimalist product photography, the design evokes a sense of "precious imperfection" that resonates with the brand's core philosophy.

Project Focus / Analysis

Scalable Design Systems & Multi-Surface Consistency.

Vivova represents a full-cycle product design approach. The focus was on creating a unified design language that functions seamlessly across vastly different contexts: a dense, data-driven desktop dashboard for internal management and a streamlined, gesture-centric mobile application for the end-user. By utilizing a shared atomic design system in Figma, I ensured that every component—from typography scales to button radii—maintained visual integrity. The interactive prototypes demonstrate complex user flows, proving that "enterprise-grade" functionality doesn't have to sacrifice aesthetic elegance.

Project Focus / Analysis

Environmental Storytelling Through Light and Shadow.

"Lovely Room" is a 3D stage build that prioritizes atmosphere over architecture. The project is an exercise in environmental storytelling—conveying the feeling of a quiet, tech-infused sanctuary after a long day. The focus was on volumetric lighting and high-precision ray-tracing to create a realistic yet stylized interior. By meticulously placing light sources (the warm TV glow vs. the cool ambient window light), I crafted a narrative of "solitary peace." Every object, from the consoles to the posters, was chosen to build a relatable persona for the room's unseen inhabitant.
